About Me

I am a quantitative researcher and engineer working at the intersection of artificial intelligence, data science, and financial markets. My background spans academic research and industry deployment of advanced AI systems. At The Ohio State University, I conducted research on machine learning for medical time-series data and multimodal large language models, including multi-agent systems and hallucination analysis. In industry, I have built large-scale agentic AI platforms for complex financial workflows, including equity research systems at Neuberger Berman and an AI-driven due-diligence platform for renewable-energy finance at CEART. I have also worked on applied quantitative research project with BlackRock and JPMorgan, developing deep learning models for rates relative-value strategies and LLM-based feature engineering pipelines for financial forecasting.

My focus is on building rigorous, production-grade AI systems for real-world financial markets, combining machine learning, market dynamics, and scalable financial infrastructure.

Projects

Short Interest Prediction

An end-to-end agentic framework for feature engineering that processes unstructured financial data and integrates FINRA short interest datasets to construct predictive models for equity short interest forecasting. Developed in collaboration with JPMorgan.

View on GitHub

MultiMedRes

A multimodal medical collaborative reasoning framework that incorporates a learner agent to proactively gain domain-specific knowledge from expert models. Uses a three-step process (Inquire, Interact, Integrate) to solve medical multimodal reasoning problems, achieving state-of-the-art zero-shot performance on X-ray visual question answering.

View on GitHub

MedVH Benchmark

A benchmark dataset for evaluating hallucination in domain-specific Large Vision Language Models within the medical context. Comprises six tasks for comprehensive understanding of textual and visual input, as well as long textual response generation.

View Dataset

FedDA

Dynamic activation strategies for clients and parameters during federated training of Heterogeneous Graph Networks. Includes a novel disentangled model (D-HGN) that enables type-oriented activation of model parameters.
